About

Mission

The mission of a food bank is to alleviate hunger and food insecurity within a community or region. Food banks work towards this mission by collecting, storing, and distributing food and grocery products to individuals and families in need.

Here are the key aspects of a food bank's mission:

1. Food Collection: Food banks gather food and grocery donations from various sources such as food manufacturers, retailers, farmers, and community food drives.These donations can include perishable and non-perishable food items.

2. Food Storage and Sorting: Food banks maintain storage facilities where donated food is sorted, inspected, and organized. This process ensures that the food is safe for consumption and can be efficiently distributed to those in need.

3. Distribution to Partner Agencies: Food banks collaborate with partner agencies, including other non-profit organization such as Tarayana Foundation Center, Happiness centre and other charitable organizations.These agencies serve as distribution points, receiving food from the food bank and providing it directly to individuals and families facing food insecurity.

4. Emergency Food Assistance: Food banks play a crucial role in responding to emergencies and natural disasters. They provide rapid assistance by mobilizing resources, coordinating with local authorities, and delivering food and supplies to affected communities.

5. Advocacy and Education: Food banks often engage in advocacy efforts to raise awareness about hunger and food insecurity issues. They work towards policy changes, collaborate with government agencies, and promote initiatives to address the root causes of hunger. Additionally, food banks may offer educational programs to help individuals and families improve their nutritional knowledge and cooking skills.

6. Collaboration and Partnerships: Food banks actively collaborate with businesses, community organizations, government entities, and volunteers to maximize their impact. These partnerships help secure food donations, funding, and other resources necessary to carry out their mission effectively. The ultimate goal of a food bank is to ensure that no one in the community goes hungry.

Vision

The vision of a Bhutan Food Bank is to create our country where no one goes hungry. A Bhutan food bank envisions a society in which all individuals and families have access to nutritious food, regardless of their circumstances or background. The primary goal is to eliminate hunger and food insecurity by distributing food to those in need and working towards long-term solutions to address the root causes of hunger.

Here are some key elements that contribute to the vision of a Bhutan food bank:

1. Hunger eradication: The ultimate vision is to eliminate hunger entirely, ensuring that everyone has enough food to lead a healthy and active life. This involves providing food assistance to individuals and families who are experiencing food insecurity.

2. Nutritional well-being: Food banks strive to provide not just any food but also nutritious food. The vision includes promoting access to a variety of fresh fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and protein sources to improve the health and well-being of those who rely on food bank services.

3. Collaboration and advocacy: Food banks envision working closely with community partners, including government agencies, nonprofits, businesses, and volunteers, to develop innovative solutions to address hunger collectively. They also engage in advocacy efforts to raise awareness about the issue of hunger, influence policy changes, and promote equitable access to food resources.

4. Sustainable solutions: Food banks seek to address the root causes of hunger and implement sustainable solutions. This may involve supporting local agriculture, reducing food waste through food recovery programs, promoting food security initiatives, and advocating for policies that address income inequality and systemic issues contributing to hungerOverall, the vision of a Bhutan food bank is to create a society in which hunger is eliminated, and everyone has access to nutritious food, leading to healthier and more resilient communities.
